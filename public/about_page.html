About the Author

Edward Brown is a wellness advocate and former software engineer whose personal transformation has inspired many. Born and raised in Milwaukee, he spent much of his early life struggling with obesity, reaching over 380 pounds at his heaviest. Despite his challenges, he built a successful career in tech, known for his analytical mind and problem-solving skills. But after a serious health scare in his early 30s, Edward realized it was time to make a change—not just physically, but mentally and emotionally. Over the course of several years, through therapy, sustainable lifestyle changes, and deep personal work, he lost over 170 pounds and redefined his relationship with health, food, and self-worth. Now in his mid-30s, Edward is the founder of Reshape Forward, a wellness initiative that helps others navigate weight loss and emotional healing with compassion and realism. He speaks at schools, community events, and wellness conferences, sharing his story to show that recovery isn’t about perfection—it’s about persistence and self-connection. With a unique blend of tech savvy, humor, and lived experience, Edward helps others believe that real change is possible when it starts from within. He currently lives in Asheville, North Carolina, with his rescue dog, Moose, is writing a memoir titled "The Weight Was Never Just the Weight.", and trying to launch an App called "NutriMotion".



The Big Idea!

Edward Brown came up with the idea for NutriMotion during the most challenging stretch of his weight loss journey. As he worked to rebuild his health, he noticed a frustrating gap in the tools available to people like him—those who weren’t just trying to count calories or hit gym goals, but who needed to build sustainable habits rooted in both movement and mindful eating. He found existing fitness apps either too rigid, overly clinical, or unmotivating. Most didn’t account for emotional eating, mental health, or the importance of small, consistent actions that feel good—not punishing. During a late-night journaling session, after a walk that helped him process a tough therapy session, the idea struck: what if there were an app that encouraged people to move intuitively and eat with intention, not perfection? He sketched out a rough concept that night—a flexible, supportive platform that would combine habit tracking, mood-based nutrition prompts, and personalized movement suggestions. Over time, this vision evolved into NutriMotion, an app designed to help users tune into their bodies, find joy in motion, and develop a healthier relationship with food and self, all without guilt or grind.

The Features!

NutriMotion is an app concept Edward Brown is currently developing, inspired by the emotional and behavioral gaps he experienced during his weight loss journey. The app is designed to support intuitive movement and mindful eating by meeting users where they are, emotionally and physically. Planned features include a mood-based movement planner that suggests activities like stretching, walking, or dancing based on energy level and emotional state, as well as a "joyful movement" reflection tool to help users focus on how they feel rather than what they achieve. On the nutrition side, Edward envisions an emotional meal journal, mood-to-food prompts, and a “check-in before you eat” tool to bring awareness to emotional eating without guilt or calorie counting. To encourage sustainable habits, the app will also feature a habit loop builder for reinforcing small daily routines, a mind-body dashboard to track mood, energy, sleep, and activity patterns (with optional wearable integration), and gamified self-compassion tools like kindness streaks and affirmation unlocks. Users will be able to connect in peer support circles based on shared goals or identities, and Edward hopes to offer optional access to certified coaches or therapists through the platform. Though still in the development phase, NutriMotion aims to be more than just a tracker—it’s intended to be a supportive, human-centered wellness companion that empowers users to transform not just their habits, but their mindset.
