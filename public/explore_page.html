People I See Everyday!



When I was at my heaviest, every walk outside felt like a reflection. I’d pass people—strangers going about their day, often carrying the same physical weight I was—and I’d feel something stir in me. Not judgment, not superiority, but recognition. I saw myself in them. The slower gaits. The way we avoided mirrors. The quiet way we made ourselves smaller in public spaces even when our bodies wouldn’t allow it. These moments weren’t about comparing or criticizing. They were about connection. Every person I saw reminded me that I wasn’t alone in this. That weight—emotional, physical, and social—was being carried by so many. And that realization sparked a change in me. Not out of shame, but out of solidarity. I wanted to live more freely, to move with less pain, to breathe easier, to reclaim joy. And I wanted to show others that it was possible to start small, to try again, and to believe in something better. The people above are the average people I have seen on street that motivated me to lose weight, and will probably motivate you to sign up as a supporting member.
